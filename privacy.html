SonarUs Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 16, 2026

SonarUs is an Apple Music social app that lets you share what you're currently playing with friends. This policy explains what data the app collects, how it is used, and the choices you have. It describes the app as it currently ships.

1. Information we collect

We collect only what we need to provide the experience:

  • Account information. When you sign in with Apple, we receive a durable Apple user identifier (your account key) and, only if you choose to share them on first sign-in, your name and email address. Apple may provide a private relay email rather than your real address — that's your choice. Your display name and (optionally) a profile picture are stored on your public profile. Your email address is stored privately and is never shown to other users.
  • Now-playing and listening data. While the app is open and you have sharing enabled, SonarUs reads the track currently playing in your device's Apple Music / system music player and your recently played history. From each track it reads the Apple Music catalog identifier, title, artist, album, and artwork image URL. SonarUs reads the music player only — it never controls playback, changes your queue, or accesses your Apple Music library, playlists, or purchase history.
  • Social data. Friend connections and follow relationships, reactions you send or receive, and songs you save.
  • Notification token. If you enable push notifications, we store a Firebase Cloud Messaging token so we can deliver alerts about friend activity.

2. How we use your information

We use your information solely to provide and operate SonarUs:

  • Show your currently playing track to your friends and followers
  • Show your friends' and followed users' listening activity in your feed
  • Enable social features such as reactions and friend / follow requests
  • Send push notifications about friend activity (only if you enable them)
  • Maintain and operate the app

We do not sell your data. We do not use your data for advertising. We do not share your data with third parties except the service providers described below. Analytics collection is disabled in this build.

3. Now-playing is foreground-only

Apple Music does not provide a way to read your currently playing track when the app is closed or in the background. SonarUs can only read and share your now-playing track while the app is open and active. We do not track your listening in the background. To keep your activity visible between sessions, the app shares a short window of your recently played history.

4. Who can see your activity

Your display name and profile picture are visible to other signed-in SonarUs users (for example, in search and friend lists). Your now-playing track and recently played history are visible only to your friends and to people who follow you. Saved songs are private to you. Your email address is never visible to other users.

5. Third-party services

SonarUs relies on the following providers:

  • Apple. Sign in with Apple handles authentication, and MusicKit / the system music player provide your now-playing and recently played data. Apple's handling of your information is governed by Apple's privacy policy.
  • Google Firebase. We use Firebase for authentication, the Cloud Firestore database, Cloud Functions, and push notifications (Firebase Cloud Messaging). Firebase processes data under Google's privacy policy. The Firebase SDK includes an analytics component, but analytics collection is disabled in this build.

We do not integrate advertising networks or data brokers.

6. Data storage and security

Your data is stored in Google Firebase (Cloud Firestore). Data in transit is encrypted via TLS. Sign-in credentials are managed by Apple and Firebase Authentication, and sensitive values on your device are kept in the iOS Keychain.

Access to your now-playing track, recently played history, and other shared data is restricted by server-side security rules so that only you and the friends and followers you've connected with can read it. While we take reasonable measures to protect your information, no method of electronic storage is 100% secure.

7. Data retention

Your recently played and listening history are used to show recent activity; the app surfaces roughly the most recent week of listening history, and older entries are not shown in the app.

Your profile, friend and follow connections, and social data are retained as long as your account is active.

When you delete your account (Settings > Delete Account), SonarUs removes your associated data — including your profile, now-playing record, recently played and listening history, reactions, saved songs, and connections — from our systems.

8. Your rights

You have the right to:

  • Access your data through the app
  • Correct your profile information at any time
  • Delete your account and all associated data (Settings > Delete Account)
  • Disable sharing at any time (Settings > Share What I'm Listening To)
  • Control or revoke music and notification permissions in iOS Settings

If you are in the European Union, you have additional rights under GDPR, including data portability and the right to object to processing.

If you are in California, you have additional rights under the CCPA, including the right to know what data we collect and the right to request deletion.

9. Children's privacy

SonarUs is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, please contact us so we can delete it.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. We will notify you of significant changes through the app. The current version of this policy is published at https://viquartucci.github.io/sonarus-legal/privacy.html. Your continued use of SonarUs after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
